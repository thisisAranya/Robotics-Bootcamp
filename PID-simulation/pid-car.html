How It Works

Click the road to create a "wind" disturbance.

  • Kp (Proportional): Reacts to the current error. A high Kp makes the car react aggressively, but can lead to overshooting and oscillations.
  • Ki (Integral): Accumulates past errors. It eliminates steady-state error, ensuring the car returns exactly to center over time. Too high, and it causes massive overshoots (wind-up).
  • Kd (Derivative): Predicts future error by looking at the rate of change. It dampens oscillations and reduces overshoot, acting like a brake as the car approaches the center line.
